## 🩺 Overview  

The **Risk Management Framework (RMF)** in **healthcare** is designed to **identify, assess, mitigate, and monitor** risks that affect **patient safety**, **data security**, and **regulatory compliance**.  

This project explores how cybersecurity principles and RMF methodology are applied to **protect healthcare systems**, **reduce vulnerabilities**, and **ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability** of sensitive patient data.  

---

## ⚙️ RMF Key Stages  

| Stage | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| 🧭 **Risk Identification** | Detect potential threats to healthcare systems, data, and patient safety. |
| 📊 **Risk Assessment** | Analyze likelihood, impact, and exposure of each risk. |
| 🛡️ **Risk Mitigation** | Apply security controls to minimize or eliminate threats. |
| 🔍 **Continuous Monitoring** | Track performance, detect anomalies, and ensure ongoing compliance. |
| 🚨 **Incident Response** | Respond swiftly to security breaches or system failures. |

---

## 💡 Benefits  

✅ **Enhanced Data Protection** – Safeguards patient records and sensitive health data  
⚕️ **Improved Patient Safety** – Reduces risks from system vulnerabilities  
📜 **Regulatory Compliance** – Meets HIPAA and healthcare cybersecurity standards  
💰 **Financial Risk Reduction** – Prevents losses from data breaches or system downtime
